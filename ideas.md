# Go-Out Landing Pages — Design Brainstorm

## Context
Three Google Ads-compliant landing pages for Go-Out products (Daily Maintenance, Extra Strength, NOW!). Target audience: men 40-65 experiencing joint discomfort related to uric acid. Must avoid all disease claims, prescription references, and trigger terms. The Go-Out brand already has a strong identity: deep navy/teal (#1B365D), golf lifestyle imagery, clean clinical feel.

## Idea 1: "Active Life" — Outdoor Editorial

**Design Movement:** Modern editorial magazine layout meets outdoor lifestyle brand (think Patagonia catalog crossed with Ritual supplements).

**Core Principles:**
1. Full-bleed lifestyle photography dominates — golf, hiking, fishing, active retirement
2. Asymmetric grid with generous negative space
3. Copy reads like a magazine feature, not a product page
4. Science sections feel like National Geographic infographics, not pharma brochures

**Color Philosophy:** Navy (#1B365D) as the authority anchor, warm sand (#E8DCC8) as the lifestyle warmth, forest green (#2D5F3A) as the natural/botanical accent. The palette says "I spend time outdoors and I take care of myself."

**Layout Paradigm:** Magazine-spread layout. Hero is a full-viewport lifestyle image with text overlay on the left third. Sections alternate between full-width imagery and contained text blocks. Ingredient cards use a horizontal scroll on mobile, stacked editorial cards on desktop.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Offset photo frames — images break out of their containers by 40px, creating depth
2. Handwritten-style annotations on ingredient diagrams (like field notes)
3. Subtle topographic map texture in background sections

**Interaction Philosophy:** Smooth parallax on hero images. Ingredient cards flip on hover to reveal mechanism of action. Sticky CTA bar slides up from bottom after 30% scroll.

**Animation:** Fade-in-up on scroll for text blocks (staggered 100ms). Hero image has a slow 15s Ken Burns zoom. Section transitions use a horizontal wipe.

**Typography System:** Headlines in Libre Baskerville (authoritative serif), body in Source Sans 3 (clean, readable). Ingredient dosages in JetBrains Mono (clinical precision).

## Idea 2: "Clinical Authority" — Science-Forward Minimalist

**Design Movement:** Scandinavian pharmaceutical design meets AG1/Ritual DTC supplement aesthetic. Think Aesop packaging translated to web.

**Core Principles:**
1. Extreme restraint — every element earns its place
2. Data visualization replaces marketing copy where possible
3. White space is the primary design element
4. Product photography is hero, not lifestyle

**Color Philosophy:** Near-white (#FAFAF8) background creates a lab-clean canvas. Deep charcoal (#1A1A1A) for authority text. A single accent — teal (#0A7E8C) — appears only on CTAs and key data points. The palette says "we have nothing to hide."

**Layout Paradigm:** Single-column narrative flow with strategic full-width breaks. No sidebar distractions. Content width maxes at 720px for body copy (optimal reading width), expanding to 1100px for ingredient grids and comparison sections. Vertical rhythm is religious — 8px base grid.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Molecular structure line art as section dividers (abstract, not literal)
2. Clinical dosage callouts in monospace type with a thin left border accent
3. Micro-data visualizations — small bar charts showing concentration comparisons

**Interaction Philosophy:** Minimal, purposeful. Accordion FAQ with smooth height animation. Ingredient cards expand inline (no modals). The page itself is the experience — no gimmicks.

**Animation:** Content fades in at 0.95 opacity to 1.0 (barely perceptible but creates polish). Scroll-triggered counter animations on key stats (847 reviews, 100,000+ customers). CTA button has a subtle pulse every 8 seconds.

**Typography System:** Headlines in DM Serif Display (warm authority without being stuffy), body in DM Sans (its natural pair). Dosages and data in IBM Plex Mono.

## Idea 3: "Golf Course Confidence" — Premium Lifestyle Brand

**Design Movement:** Luxury golf brand meets premium wellness (think Trackman meets Momentous supplements). Dark mode with strategic warm accents.

**Core Principles:**
1. Dark backgrounds signal premium positioning and differentiation from every other supplement page
2. Golf and active lifestyle imagery throughout — this is the audience
3. Testimonials are the backbone, woven throughout (not dumped at bottom)
4. The subscription offer feels like joining a club, not buying pills

**Color Philosophy:** Deep navy-black (#0D1B2A) as the dominant canvas. Warm gold (#C9A84C) as the premium accent — it catches the eye like a golf trophy. Soft cream (#F5F0E8) for text. The palette says "this is for people who invest in themselves."

**Layout Paradigm:** Full-width dark sections with contained light-on-dark content. Hero uses a split — left side is the value proposition on dark, right side is a product shot with dramatic lighting. Sections use alternating dark/slightly-lighter-dark backgrounds to create depth without color changes.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Gold accent lines — thin 1px gold rules that frame key content blocks
2. Dramatic product photography with rim lighting on dark backgrounds
3. Testimonial cards with a subtle gold left border and italic pull quotes

**Interaction Philosophy:** Hover states reveal gold underlines and subtle glow effects. The subscription selector uses a golf-scorecard metaphor (frequency options laid out like hole selections). Smooth scroll-to-section navigation.

**Animation:** Hero text types in character by character (typewriter effect). Gold accent lines draw themselves on scroll. Product images fade in with a slight scale-up (1.02 to 1.0). Section backgrounds have a very subtle gradient shift on scroll.

**Typography System:** Headlines in Cormorant Garamond (elegant, high-end serif), body in Outfit (modern geometric sans). Price/dosage callouts in Space Grotesk (technical but warm).

## Selected Approach: Idea 2 — "Clinical Authority"

**Rationale:** For Google Ads landing pages, conversion rate is everything. The science-forward minimalist approach:
1. Builds trust fastest — the clean, clinical aesthetic signals "we're legitimate, not a scammy supplement brand"
2. Loads fastest — minimal imagery means faster page speed, better Quality Score
3. Converts best for this demo — men 40-65 respond to data and authority, not lifestyle aspiration
4. Safest for Google Ads — the restrained copy style naturally avoids trigger terms
5. Differentiates from go-out.life — the main site is lifestyle-forward; the landing pages should feel like the science behind the brand

However, I'll incorporate the golf lifestyle hero imagery from Idea 3 to maintain brand connection and the warm editorial feel from Idea 1 for the testimonial sections. The base is clinical authority with strategic warmth.
